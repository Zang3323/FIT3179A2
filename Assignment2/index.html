Unveiling Population trends across the world in 2012


Relationship between life expectancy and adult mortality in countries

The chart indicates the relationship between life expectancy and adult mortality in countries for the year 2012. As life expectancy increases, adult mortality decreases, indicating a negative correlation. Most developing countries are spread out in the chart, having a wider range of life expectancy and adult mortality rate, with mortality rates as high as 513 per 1000 people. That's a lot! In comparison, developed countries cluster in the lower-right corner, signifying both high life expectancy and lower adult mortality rates. This pattern underscores the impact of development status on these vital health indicators.

In the dataset analyzing the years from 2003 to 2012, we observe variations in the ratio of males to females across different countries.
